Regular Class Aircraft – Team NOVA

SAE Drone Development Competition (DDC) 2024–25
Mar Athanasius College of Engineering, Kothamangalam

Project Overview

Team NOVA represents the Regular Class division of the Aero Club for the SAE Drone Development Competition (DDC) 2024–25. This category focuses on endurance, aerodynamic efficiency, and payload optimization in large-scale drone design.

Our aircraft is a fixed-wing UAV designed for high-lift efficiency under payload conditions. The project highlights innovation, collaboration, and practical application of aeronautical design principles fostered by the Aero Club.

Mission Objective

To engineer and test a high‑efficiency regular‑class aircraft capable of stable autonomous flight, optimal payload carrying capacity, and superior aerodynamic performance in mission profiles under SAE DDC 2024–25.

Technical Highlights

  • Aircraft Type: Fixed‑Wing UAV
  • Configuration: High‑lift blended‑wing design
  • Material: Carbon fiber composites and balsa wood assemblies
  • Control System: Advanced PID tuning for autonomous stability
  • Category: Regular Class | SAE DDC 2024–25

About the Team

Team NOVA consists of passionate and skilled engineering students from Mar Athanasius College of Engineering, Kothamangalam. With expertise in aerodynamics and UAV systems, the team embodies innovation and dedication to academic excellence.

Aero Club Legacy

The Aero Club of MACE has consistently showcased prototypes under both Regular and Micro Class categories of SAE DDC. With Team NOVA (2024–25), we continue our pursuit of excellence in aircraft design and innovation.
